Setting up your Google Business Profile

This is the single most important step to appear in "yoga near me" and Google Maps results across West London. Everything below is pre-written with your details, just copy, paste and click. Work through it in one sitting.

1

Start your profile

Go to business.google.com and sign in with the Google account you want to manage this from (ideally a business Gmail, not a personal one, if you have it). Click "Manage now" then "Add your business."

Business name
soniayogi

Use exactly this, the same name everywhere online helps Google trust you. Don't add keywords like "yoga West London" to the name, it breaks Google's rules.

2

Choose your categories

Set the primary category first, then add the others as additional categories. These decide which searches you show up for.

Yoga instructor — primary Yoga studio Meditation instructor Meditation center Wellness program

"Yoga instructor" fits you best as a teacher who travels to clients. You can refine these any time.

3

Set your location type

Google will ask "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?" Because you teach in clients' homes, online and at venues rather than a public studio, choose No, this makes you a service-area business. Your home address stays private; only your service area shows.

When asked for your address (kept private, for verification only):

Your W4 1LB address
London, United Kingdom

6

Verify your business

Google needs to confirm you're real. Depending on what they offer you, this is by postcard (a code mailed to your address, 5 to 14 days), phone, email, or video. Choose whatever's available and complete it, your profile won't show publicly until you do.

If offered video verification, it's the fastest, have your phone ready to film your teaching space and a prop or two.

Once verified — fill it out fully

A complete profile ranks far better than a bare one. Paste these in under "Edit profile."

7

10

Add photos

Profiles with photos get far more clicks. Upload 8 to 12 good images, you already have them on your site:

  • Your portrait / logo as the profile photo
  • You teaching, in flow, meditating (the ones from your homepage)
  • The retreat venue shots
  • Add a few fresh ones over time, Google rewards activity

The secret weapon: reviews

After every class or private session with a happy client, ask them to leave a Google review. Reviews are one of the top three local ranking factors, and they're what convince new clients to book.

Google gives you a short "review link" you can text or email, send it with a warm one-liner: "It would mean a lot if you'd share a few words about your practice with me."

After Google Business Profile

Once your profile is live, these compound your local visibility:

  • Google Search Console — verify soniayogi.com and submit your sitemap (https://soniayogi.com/sitemap.xml) so Google indexes the site.
  • Get listed consistently — Yoga Alliance teacher directory, local Chiswick & West London business listings, yoga apps. Use the exact same name, phone and website everywhere.
  • Keep the profile active — post updates, photos or class news every couple of weeks. Active profiles rank higher.
  • Gather reviews steadily — a few each month beats a sudden burst.
  • Link your Instagram — @sonia.alopezyogi, social signals reinforce your presence.
